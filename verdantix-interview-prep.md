## 🎯 Top 10 Likely Questions + My Answers

### 1. "Walk us through your end-to-end recruitment process"

**My Answer:**
> "I own the full cycle: briefing → sourcing → screening → interviewing → offer → onboarding. For a typical analyst role, I start with a 30-min briefing with the hiring manager to define must-haves vs. nice-to-haves. I then build a Boolean search string and tap into 3-4 channels: LinkedIn Recruiter, niche job boards, employee referrals, and direct outreach. I screen within 48 hours, coordinate 2-3 interview rounds, and manage offer negotiations. My average time-to-hire is [X] days with [Y]% offer acceptance."

**Key points to hit:**
- Structure (show you're organized)
- Speed (they want efficiency)
- Quality (not just filling reqs)
- Metrics (data-driven mindset)

### 2. "How have you used AI in recruiting?"

**My Answer:**
> "[Pick 1-2 tools you've actually used or researched]"

**If you have experience:**
> "I used Gem for sequenced outreach—wrote 3 InMail variants, A/B tested, and saw 3x reply rates vs. manual sending. I also used LinkedIn's AI suggestions to find passive candidates I'd missed."

**If you're new to AI:**

---

### 3. "How do you manage multiple vacancies at once?"

**My Answer:**
> "I prioritize by business impact. I use a simple framework:
> - **Tier 1:** Revenue-facing or critical roles (fill first)
> - **Tier 2:** Important but can wait 2-3 weeks
> - **Tier 3:** Nice-to-have or backfill roles
> 
> I also batch similar activities—sourcing Mondays, interviews Tue-Thu, offers Fridays. This keeps me focused and candidates moving."

---

### 4. "How do you maximize LinkedIn Recruiter?"

**My Answer:**
> "I use it 3 ways:
> 1. **Boolean searches** - I layer title, skills, company, and location filters
> 2. **Talent pools** - I save searches and get weekly alerts on new matches
> 3. **InMail optimization** - I A/B test subject lines and keep messages under 150 words
> 
> My InMail response rate is [X]%, which is [above/below] industry benchmark."

---

### 5. "Describe a time you had to manage a difficult hiring manager"

**Use STAR method:**
> **Situation:** Hiring manager rejected all 10 candidates in 2 months
> **Task:** Recalibrate expectations and fill the role
> **Action:** I pulled market data (salary benchmarks, candidate availability), showed them the funnel drop-off points, and we rewrote the JD together
> **Result:** Filled the role in 3 weeks with candidate #11 (who matched the revised profile)

### 6. "How do you ensure great candidate experience?"

**My Answer:**
> "Candidates are future clients or brand ambassadors. I:
> - Respond within 48 hours (even rejections)
> - Give interview feedback within 24 hours
> - Send a candidate NPS survey post-process
> - Train hiring managers on interview best practices
> 
> At [past company], our candidate NPS was [X]/10."

### 7. "What recruiting metrics do you track?"

**My Answer:**
> "I track a full funnel:
> - **Top:** Applications, source quality
> - **Middle:** Screen→interview conversion, interview→offer
> - **Bottom:** Offer acceptance, time-to-hire
> - **Quality:** 90-day retention, hiring manager satisfaction
> 
> I report monthly to leadership and use data to optimize sourcing channels."

---

### 8. "How would you source a niche research analyst?"

**My Answer:**
> "Niche roles need creative channels:
> - **Publications:** Find authors of industry reports
> - **Conferences:** Speaker lists from Transform, EHS events
> - **Competitors:** Map analysts at Verdantix competitors
> - **Universities:** PhD programs in sustainability/energy
> - **Referrals:** Ask placed candidates for peer referrals
> 
> I'd also partner with marketing to amplify employer brand in those communities."

**My Answer:**

> - Source from diverse talent pools (SeekOut, POC communities)
> - Ensure diverse interview panels
> - Track diverse funnel drop-off points
> 
> At [past company], we increased diverse hires from [X]% to [Y]% in [Z] months."
