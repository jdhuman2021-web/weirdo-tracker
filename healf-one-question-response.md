### Final Draft Response (~290 words)

**The Candidate Everyone Else Passed On**

At KPMG, I was recruiting for a Tech Assurance Assistant Manager role. Scrolling LinkedIn, a post caught my eye. The profile showed relevant experience plus volunteer work—signal, not noise.

I sent a connection request with a note about their post. Accepted overnight. We chatted.

**What I saw:** Energy. Willingness to learn. Builder intensity. But the candidate was "too junior"—their salary was 70% below the role's band. Everyone else would have passed.

My gut said: potential before proof.

I explained what KPMG looked for. They doubted themselves—felt the role was too senior. I pushed back: "You have the background. It's a step up, but you're ready."

Silence for three days. No application. I followed up. Walked them through KPMG values they aligned with. Gave more encouragement.

The process took two months. We stayed in contact. I pushed the hiring team for decisions. Watching them grow in confidence was the real signal.

**Outcome:** They got the offer. 70% salary uplift. Incredible feedback from the hiring team.

**What happened after:** Still at KPMG. Promoted to Programme Manager. And this message on LinkedIn:

> *"Jonathan was integral to me getting a position in the Big 4. He helped me to aim higher and see potential in myself, enabling me to meet my career goals, he was like my recruitment fairy godmother. Thanks for believing in me Jonathan!"*

**Why I saw it:** I don't read CVs first. I read energy, trajectory, choices that signal what they're running toward. Salary is noise. Volunteer work is signal.

**The lesson:** Potential isn't hidden. It's visible in the things people choose when no one's watching. My job is to notice before the market does.

That's what I'll do at Healf. Not find candidates. Find inevitability.

### Draft Response (~290 words)

**The Wellbeing Intelligence Summit**

Three months. No job boards. No InMails. No recruiters.

Here's what I build:

**Month 1: The Magnet**

Launch the Wellbeing Intelligence Summit—a one-day event bringing together 50 operators from prestige firms (BCG, McKinsey, Goldman) who are privately questioning their next chapter.

Not a hiring event. A conversation about what's next in wellbeing. Speakers: Healf founders, portfolio CEOs, wellbeing scientists.

The content is the filter. People who apply are already signal-reading themselves.

**Month 2: The Programme**

Announce the Velocity Programme—a 6-week sprint for career changers. Not training. Proof-of-work. Participants ship a real Healf project (category research, vendor analysis, growth experiment).

Completion = interview. No CV review. The work is the credential.

**Month 3: The Network**

Every Summit attendee and Velocity participant becomes a node. They bring two peers next time. The pond fills itself.

**Why this works:**

- **Self-selection:** Only people ready to move apply
- **Signal over noise:** We see how they think, not what their CV says
- **Network effects:** Each cohort recruits the next
- **Identity alignment:** They're already living the Four Pillars™—this is their identity expressed as business

**The metric:**

Not applications. Conversion. Of 50 Summit attendees, 10 join Velocity, 3 join Healf. All three raise the bar.

**The principle:**

You don't fish with a rod. You build a pond. The pond I'd build makes extraordinary people come to Healf before they're looking.

That's the system. That's the job.
